# 3.1 Building Teams

**ECO Task**: Build a team

Building a high-performing project team is more art than science. For the 2026 PMP exam, it's about deliberately setting the conditions for success, anticipating common pitfalls, and understanding the organizational context.

---

## The Construction Phases of Team Building

Team building isn't a single event; it's a continuous process with distinct phases.

1. **Formation (Acquisition & Onboarding)**
 * **PMP Focus**: Strategic staffing, resource negotiation, skills matrix, RACI charting, and Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI).
 * **Key Concept**: Getting the right people with the right skills and establishing clear roles and responsibilities.
2. **Development (Growth & Performance)**
 * **PMP Focus**: Fostering team cohesion, psychological safety, managing team dynamics, and continuous improvement.
 * **Key Concept**: Enabling the team to evolve into a high-performing unit.
3. **Agreements (Chartering & Ground Rules)**
 * **PMP Focus**: Establishing clear working agreements, decision-making processes, conflict resolution, and communication norms.
 * **Key Concept**: Creating a shared understanding of how the team will operate.
4. **Virtual Leadership (Adapting to New Realities)**
 * **PMP Focus**: Managing distributed teams, leveraging technology, and addressing cultural nuances (e.g., Hofstede's dimensions).
 * **Key Concept**: Leading effectively in hybrid and remote environments.

---

## Psychological Safety: The Foundation

Google's Project Aristotle identified **Psychological Safety** as the single most important factor for high-performing teams.

* **Definition**: A shared belief held by members of a team that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking. It's about feeling safe to:
 * Speak up with ideas.
 * Admit mistakes.
 * Ask questions.
 * Challenge the status quo.
 * Be vulnerable.

* **PM's Role**:
 * **Model Vulnerability**: Admit your own mistakes, ask for help.
 * **Invite Participation**: Actively solicit input from all team members.
 * **Respond Constructively**: Don't punish failure; treat it as a learning opportunity.
 * **Emphasize Learning**: Frame challenges as experiments.
 * **Protect the Team**: Shield the team from external blame.

## The PMI Lens: Acquire, Develop, Manage the Team

The PMP exam frequently frames questions around these core processes.

### 1. Acquire Project Team

* **Skills Matrix**: A grid displaying required skills for a project vs. available skills of potential team members. Used for gap analysis.
* **Virtual Teams**: Selecting team members regardless of geographical location. Requires explicit communication plans.
* **Negotiation**: Project managers often need to negotiate for resources with functional managers or other project managers.
* **Pre-Assignment**: When certain team members are selected in advance (e.g., in a matrix organization or due to specialized skills).
* **Colocation**: Physically placing the team members in the same location to enhance communication and collaboration.

### 2. Develop Project Team

* **Team Building Activities**: From casual social gatherings to formal workshops, designed to improve interpersonal relationships and team cohesion.
* **Training**: Providing team members with necessary skills. Can be formal, informal, on-the-job.
* **Recognition & Rewards**: Motivating team members and acknowledging good performance. Must be timely and based on clearly defined criteria.
* **Feedback**: Regular, constructive feedback to improve individual and team performance.
* **Emotional Intelligence (EQ)**: Fostering self-awareness, self-regulation, motivation, empathy, and social skills within the team.
* **Coaching & Mentoring**: Supporting individual growth and development.

### 3. Manage Project Team

* **Conflict Resolution**: Addressing disagreements and facilitating healthy resolutions.
* **Performance Appraisals**: Formal assessments of individual and team performance.
* **Decision Making**: Facilitating effective team decision-making processes.
* **Issue Log**: Tracking and resolving team-related issues.
* **Interpersonal Skills**: Using communication, leadership, negotiation, and influence to guide the team.

## Week 1: Team Setup Checklist (Preventing People-Domain Problems)

This is what a Project Manager does in the early stages to proactively prevent team issues.

| Item | Description | Output |
| :--------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------ |
| **Kick-off Meeting** | Formal start, vision, goals, roles, and initial team building. | Shared understanding, team directory |
| **Team Charter Session** | Develop working agreements, ground rules, decision model, conflict resolution. | Approved Team Charter |
| **Skills Matrix Review** | Confirm team skills, identify gaps, plan training/mentoring. | Updated Skills Matrix, Training Plan |
| **RACI Charting (Key Deliverables)** | Clarify ownership: Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed. | Draft RACI Chart for key processes |
| **Communication Plan Initial Draft** | Define "how we communicate" (tools, cadence, norms). | Initial Communication Matrix |
| **Onboarding Check-in (Individuals)** | One-on-one with each team member to set expectations, understand needs. | Individual Development Plans (optional) |
| **Psychological Safety Discussion** | Openly discuss importance of speaking up, making mistakes safely. | Explicit agreement on team values |
| **Virtual Team Working Agreements** | For distributed teams: agree on meeting times, response expectations, tools. | Virtual Team Guidelines |

::: tip Exam Mindset
Proactive planning and upfront agreement on how the team will operate (via a Team Charter) are critical. Don't wait for problems to emerge; establish the foundation for a high-performing team from Day 1.
:::
