# PMP 2026 Global Glossary (A-Z)

#exam-prep

Master the standardized language of project management. This glossary aligns with **PMBOK 8th Edition** and the **2026 PMP Exam Content Outline (ECO)**.

---

<div class="glossary-section">

## A

::: details Acceptance Criteria
Measurable conditions that must be met for deliverables to be accepted by stakeholders. In agile contexts, often part of the Definition of Done.
:::

::: details Adaptive Approach
See: **Agile Approach**
:::

::: details Agile Approach
An iterative and incremental delivery approach that emphasizes flexibility, collaboration, customer feedback, and continuous improvement. Includes frameworks such as Scrum, Kanban, XP, and others.
:::

::: details Assumption
A factor considered to be true, real, or certain without proof or demonstration. Assumptions carry risk if they prove to be false.
:::

## B

::: details BAC (Budget at Completion)
The total planned budget for the project or work package. Used in Earned Value Management calculations.
:::

::: details Backlog
An ordered list of work items, features, or requirements to be completed. In agile contexts, typically maintained by the Product Owner and prioritized by business value.
:::

::: details Baseline
An approved version of a work product (scope, schedule, cost) that can be changed only through formal change control procedures. Used as a reference point for performance measurement.
:::

::: details Benefit
A measurable improvement resulting from an outcome that is perceived as an advantage by one or more stakeholders. Benefits contribute to value.
:::

::: details Benefits Realization
The process of ensuring that project outcomes deliver the expected benefits defined in the business case. Includes planning, tracking, and measuring benefits during and after project delivery.
:::

::: details Business Case
A documented economic feasibility study used to establish validity of the benefits of a selected component lacking sufficient definition and that is used as a basis for the authorization of further project management activities.
:::

::: details Business Environment Domain
One of three PMP exam domains (**26% of exam**). Focuses on strategy alignment, benefits and value, compliance, governance, and sustainability.
:::

## C

::: details CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management)
PMI's entry-level project management certification for those with less experience than required for PMP.
:::

::: details Change Control
A process whereby modifications to documents, deliverables, or baselines are identified, documented, approved, or rejected.
:::

::: details Change Management
The process of transitioning individuals, teams, and organizations from a current state to a desired future state to realize benefits. Includes communication, training, and adoption strategies.
:::

::: details Constraint
A limiting factor that affects the execution of a project, program, portfolio, or process. Common constraints include scope, schedule, cost, quality, resources, and risk.
:::

::: details Cost Performance Index (CPI)
An Earned Value Management metric calculated as **EV / AC**. Indicates cost efficiency: CPI > 1.0 means under budget; CPI < 1.0 means over budget.
:::

::: details Critical Path
The sequence of activities that determines the minimum project duration. Activities on the critical path have zero float.
:::

## D

::: details Definition of Done (DoD)
A shared understanding of what it means for work to be complete. In agile contexts, includes quality criteria, testing requirements, and acceptance standards.
:::

::: details Deliverable
Any unique and verifiable product, result, or capability to perform a service that is required to be produced to complete a process, phase, or project.
:::

::: details Dependency
A relationship between two activities or between an activity and a milestone. Types include finish-to-start, start-to-start, finish-to-finish, and start-to-finish.
:::

## E

::: details EAC (Estimate at Completion)
The expected total cost of completing all work. Common formula: **BAC / CPI**.
:::

::: details Earned Value (EV)
The measure of work performed expressed in terms of the budget authorized for that work. A key metric in Earned Value Management.
:::

::: details Earned Value Management (EVM)
A methodology that combines scope, schedule, and resource measurements to assess project performance and progress. Uses metrics like CPI, SPI, EAC, and TCPI.
:::

::: details ECO (Exam Content Outline)
PMI's official document defining the structure, content, and tasks for the PMP certification exam. Updated periodically to reflect current practice.
:::

::: details Escalation
The process of engaging higher levels of authority or management to resolve issues, make decisions, or provide resources beyond the project manager's authority.
:::

## F

::: details Float (Slack)
The amount of time an activity can be delayed without delaying the project finish date (total float) or a successor activity (free float).
:::

## G

::: details Governance
The framework, functions, and processes that guide project management activities to create value aligned with organizational strategy. Includes decision-making structures, policies, and oversight.
:::

## H

::: details Hybrid Approach
A combination of predictive and agile approaches tailored to the project context. May use predictive planning with agile execution, or phase-based delivery with iterative development within phases.
:::

## I

::: details Increment
A functional, tested, and potentially shippable addition to the product. In Scrum, produced at the end of each Sprint.
:::

::: details Issue
A current condition or situation that may impact project objectives. Unlike risks (which are uncertain future events), issues are happening now and require immediate attention.
:::

::: details Iteration
A timeboxed cycle of development work. In agile contexts, synonymous with Sprint in Scrum.
:::

## K

::: details Kanban
An agile approach that visualizes work, limits work in progress, and focuses on flow and continuous delivery. Uses a Kanban board to track work items through stages.
:::

## L

::: details Lessons Learned
Knowledge gained during a project that shows how project events were addressed or should be addressed in the future to improve performance. Captured throughout the project and formally documented at closure.
:::

## M

::: details Milestone
A significant point or event in a project. Milestones have zero duration and are used to mark phase completions, decision points, or deliverable submissions.
:::

::: details Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
The smallest version of a product that can be released to gather validated learning about customers with the least effort.
:::

## O

::: details Outcome
The end result or consequence of a process or project. Outcomes describe changes in behavior or conditions that result from using project outputs. Outcomes enable benefits.
:::

::: details Output
A tangible or intangible artifact, product, service, or result produced by a project. Outputs enable outcomes.
:::

## P

::: details People Domain
One of three PMP exam domains (**33% of exam**). Focuses on leadership, team development, stakeholder engagement, communication, and conflict resolution.
:::

::: details PMBOK (Project Management Body of Knowledge)
PMI's foundational standard that provides guidelines and best practices for project management. Currently in **8th Edition** (released November 2025).
:::

::: details PMI (Project Management Institute)
The global professional association for project management that administers the PMP certification and publishes project management standards.
:::

::: details PMP (Project Management Professional)
PMI's globally recognized certification for experienced project managers. Validates knowledge and experience in leading and directing projects.
:::

::: details Portfolio
A collection of projects, programs, subsidiary portfolios, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.
:::

::: details Predictive Approach
A project delivery approach that emphasizes detailed upfront planning, defined scope, and sequential execution. Also known as waterfall or plan-driven approach.
:::

::: details Process Domain
One of three PMP exam domains (**41% of exam**). Focuses on planning, executing, monitoring, controlling, and closing work across all delivery approaches.
:::

::: details Product Backlog
An ordered list of everything that is known to be needed in the product. In Scrum, maintained by the Product Owner.
:::

::: details Product Owner
In Scrum and other agile frameworks, the person responsible for maximizing the value of the product and managing the Product Backlog.
:::

::: details Program
A group of related projects, subsidiary programs, and program activities managed in a coordinated manner to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.
:::

::: details Progressive Elaboration
The iterative process of increasing the level of detail in a project management plan as greater amounts of information and more accurate estimates become available.
:::

::: details Project
A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. Projects drive organizational change and deliver value.
:::

::: details Project Charter
A document issued by the project sponsor that formally authorizes the existence of a project and provides the project manager with authority to apply organizational resources to project activities.
:::

::: details Project Management Office (PMO)
An organizational structure that standardizes project-related governance processes and facilitates the sharing of resources, methodologies, tools, and techniques.
:::

## Q

::: details Quality
The degree to which a set of inherent characteristics fulfills requirements. Includes both fitness for use (does it meet stakeholder needs?) and conformance to requirements (does it meet specifications?).
:::

## R

::: details RACI Matrix
A responsibility assignment matrix that defines roles as Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, or Informed for project activities and deliverables.
:::

::: details Release
A version of a product or service made available to customers or users. In agile contexts, may occur at the end of one or more iterations.
:::

::: details Requirements
Conditions or capabilities that must be met or possessed by a deliverable to satisfy a contract, standard, specification, or other formally imposed document.
:::

::: details Retrospective
A regular meeting where the team reflects on their process and identifies improvements. In Scrum, held at the end of each Sprint.
:::

::: details Risk
An uncertain event or condition that, if it occurs, has a positive or negative effect on project objectives. Includes both threats (negative risks) and opportunities (positive risks).
:::

::: details Risk Register
A repository that captures identified risks, their characteristics, potential responses, and current status.
:::

::: details Roadmap
A high-level strategic plan that shows major deliverables, milestones, and dependencies over time. Used in product management and program management.
:::

## S

::: details Schedule Performance Index (SPI)
An Earned Value Management metric calculated as **EV / PV**. Indicates schedule efficiency: SPI > 1.0 means ahead of schedule; SPI < 1.0 means behind schedule.
:::

::: details Scope
The sum of the products, services, and results to be provided as a project. Includes both product scope (features and functions) and project scope (work to deliver the product).
:::

::: details Scope Creep
The uncontrolled expansion of product or project scope without adjustments to time, cost, and resources.
:::

::: details Scrum
An agile framework for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products. Uses fixed-length Sprints, defined roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Scrum Team), and ceremonies (Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective).
:::

::: details Scrum Master
In Scrum, the person responsible for promoting and supporting Scrum by helping everyone understand Scrum theory, practices, rules, and values. A servant leader for the Scrum Team.
:::

::: details Servant Leadership
A leadership philosophy where the leader's primary goal is to serve the team by removing obstacles, providing resources, and enabling team success.
:::

::: details Sprint
A timeboxed iteration in Scrum, typically 1-4 weeks, during which a potentially shippable product increment is created.
:::

::: details Stakeholder
An individual, group, or organization that may affect, be affected by, or perceive itself to be affected by a decision, activity, or outcome of a project, program, or portfolio.
:::

::: details Stakeholder Engagement
The process of communicating and working with stakeholders to meet their needs and expectations, address issues, and foster appropriate stakeholder involvement.
:::

::: details Story Points
A unit of measure used in agile estimation to express the overall effort required to fully implement a user story or other work item. Relative rather than absolute.
:::

::: details Sustainability
The ability to meet the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. Includes environmental, social, and economic dimensions.
:::

## T

::: details TCPI (To-Complete Performance Index)
An Earned Value Management metric that indicates the cost performance required on remaining work to meet a specified budget goal. Formula: **(BAC - EV) / (BAC - AC)** for BAC target.
:::

::: details Tailoring
Determining the appropriate combination of processes, inputs, tools, techniques, outputs, and life cycle phases to manage a project based on its unique context.
:::

::: details Triple Constraint
The traditional view of project constraints as scope, time, and cost. Modern practice recognizes additional constraints including quality, resources, and risk.
:::

## U

::: details User Story
A brief description of a feature or requirement from an end-user perspective. Typically follows the format: "As a [role], I want [feature], so that [benefit]."
:::

## V

::: details Value
The worth, importance, or usefulness of something to stakeholders. Includes financial returns, strategic positioning, customer satisfaction, social impact, and other dimensions.
:::

::: details Velocity
In agile contexts, a measure of the amount of work a team completes during a Sprint or iteration. Used for planning future iterations.
:::

## W

::: details WBS (Work Breakdown Structure)
A hierarchical decomposition of the total scope of work to be carried out by the project team to accomplish project objectives and create required deliverables.
:::

::: details Waterfall
See: **Predictive Approach**
:::
